The rules behind political ads in Europe

A measurement study, not an opinion: what happens to a party's visibility once attention shifts from TV to social media.

Belgium's TV rule, and a platform with no such rule

Belgian broadcast media apply a professional rule known as the cordon médiatique. It denies certain parties, currently the Francophone far-right party Chez Nous, direct access to live TV and radio. Meta's ad platform has no equivalent gatekeeping. For a master's thesis, I studied 172 Meta ads from six Francophone Belgian parties, covering 1 September 2023 through the 9 June 2024 federal election, to measure whether that gap in the rules changes who actually reaches voters. The study measures visibility conditions only. It does not take a position on whether the cordon médiatique is good policy, and it does not measure vote choice or persuasion.

What the numbers show

Chez Nous scored the highest visibility deficit of the six parties on a composite index built from six standardized variables, despite running the most ads in the sample. Its ads also converted the least efficiently of any party, at 278 impressions per euro spent, against 413 for the Parti Socialiste and 432 for MR. Ward clustering splits an "institutional" group, PS, MR, and DéFI, with bigger budgets and more diverse ad formats, from a "marginal" group that includes Chez Nous. So a broadcast rule that keeps a party off television does not automatically translate into an equal or efficient reach online, even on a platform with no editorial gate.

Real reach, real difficulties

A shift in attention from TV to social media does open a channel that broadcast rules cannot touch, even with some real difficulties attached to it. Content coding of the same 172 ads found Chez Nous the only party running a substantial share of immigration-themed ads, 12 of its 45, a narrower message mix than competitors spread across mobilization, environment, and economic themes. Combined with the lowest spend and the weakest per-euro reach in the dataset, the picture is a door left open rather than a clean path through it. Whether that gap closes as spending and formats catch up is an open question the current data cannot answer on its own.

Sources: 172 Meta Ad Library ads, six Francophone Belgian parties, 1 Sept 2023 - 9 June 2024, collected and analyzed for a supervised master's thesis (Data Science & Business Intelligence, EDC Paris Business School). Full dataset and methodology in the Belgium-political-ads-visibility repo on GitHub.
