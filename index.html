Data Strategy · International Trade

Brexit & the global automotive industry

Overview

A dissertation project at the University of Westminster. It studied how Brexit changed the global automotive industry, from supply chains to trade rules.

Challenge

Brexit changed tariffs, trade rules, and supply routes fast. Automakers needed new data to plan production and sales across borders.

Data

Statista datasets on vehicle production, trade, and tariffs, alongside government and industry reports on regulation changes.

Methodology

I compared trade and tariff data before and after Brexit. I tracked how manufacturers shifted supply chains and adjusted to new standards across countries.

Key insights

  • Tariff changes hit specific vehicle categories harder than others.
  • Manufacturers rerouted supply chains to cut new customs costs.
  • Standardization gaps slowed cross-border parts trade.
  • Sales patterns shifted fastest in markets most exposed to UK-EU trade.

Technologies

Trade Data Analysis · Statista · Economic Modeling · Cross-Country Comparison

Impact

The findings show how one political decision moves an entire industry. Manufacturers, regulators, and investors can use this kind of analysis to plan for the next trade shock.

University dissertation. View the code on GitHub →

Data Strategy · Workforce Analytics

Diversity in the African insurance industry

Overview

A research project with EY People Consulting, FANAF, and the IFC. It looked at diversity across the African insurance sector.

Challenge

Insurance companies across Africa had scattered, often incomplete data on gender and diversity in their workforce. Leaders needed a clear read to act on.

Data

Qualitative and quantitative data from insurance companies and industry bodies across multiple African countries.

Methodology

I collected, cleaned, and analyzed workforce datasets. I combined survey responses with company data to spot patterns in hiring, promotion, and retention.

Key insights

  • Women held fewer senior roles than their share of the workforce would predict.
  • Retention gaps showed up strongest in technical and actuarial roles.
  • A handful of companies had practices worth copying across the sector.
  • Data quality varied a lot by country, which limited some comparisons.

Technologies

Qualitative & Quantitative Analysis · Data Cleaning · Stakeholder Reporting

Impact

The team turned these findings into recommendations for FANAF and IFC stakeholders, giving insurers a data-backed case for changing hiring and promotion practices.

Internal research project, not publicly linked. Related public project: Insurance-in-Africa on GitHub →

AI & Machine Learning · Healthcare

Deep learning for medical image analysis

Overview

A university AI project. I built and tested neural networks that read chest X-rays.

Challenge

Radiologists read huge volumes of X-rays by eye. A model that flags likely pneumonia or cancer cases could speed up that work.

Data

Labeled X-ray image datasets covering healthy patients and cases of pneumonia and certain cancers.

Methodology

I built a Multi-Layer Perceptron and a Convolutional Neural Network, then compared their accuracy on the same image sets. I tuned each model and measured how well it told sick scans from healthy ones.

Key insights

  • The CNN outperformed the MLP on raw image classification.
  • Image preprocessing changed accuracy more than small changes in network depth.
  • False negatives dropped when I added more training examples of rare cases.

Technologies

Python · Convolutional Neural Networks · Multi-Layer Perceptrons · Medical Imaging

Impact

The project shows how far a well-trained model can go with a limited image set, and points to where AI can support radiologists rather than replace their judgment.

University research project. View the code on GitHub →

Data Strategy · Political Analysis

Political advertising analytics in Belgium

Overview

A research project on political advertising in Belgium. I used clustering to study how campaigns target voters.

Challenge

Political ads reach different audiences depending on the platform and the message. Regulators and campaigns both need to understand how targeting actually works.

Data

Political advertising datasets and Belgium's regulatory framework for campaign transparency.

Methodology

I applied clustering and audience segmentation to the ad data, then compared campaign targeting choices against Belgium's disclosure rules to see where practice matched policy.

Key insights

  • Distinct voter clusters received measurably different ad messages from the same campaign.
  • Some targeting patterns sat right at the edge of what disclosure rules required.
  • Regional language differences shaped targeting more than age or income data.

Technologies

Data Clustering · Audience Segmentation · Regulatory Analysis

Impact

The findings show regulators where transparency rules do and don't match campaign behavior, a read that matters more as political spending moves online.

Research project. View the code on GitHub →

Data Strategy · Marketing Analytics

Meta ads benchmarking for perfume brands

Overview

A personal project building a scraping and benchmarking framework for Meta ad activity across perfume brands.

Challenge

Brands need a repeatable way to compare ad cadence, spend, and creative approach across competitors, but most benchmarking work is still done by hand.

Data

A demo dataset covering six fictional perfume brands, built to test the pipeline before pointing it at real Meta Ad Library data.

Methodology

I built a clustering and benchmarking pipeline that groups brands by ad cadence and spend pattern, then ranks them on a composite score.

Key insights

  • A repeatable framework can benchmark spend and cadence across any brand set, real or synthetic.
  • Clustering surfaces natural brand archetypes without manual tagging.
  • The pipeline is ready to run on real Meta Ad Library data with no structural changes.

Technologies

Python · pandas · Hierarchical Clustering · PCA

Impact

The framework gives a marketing team a starting point to benchmark competitors' ad activity systematically instead of reading the Ad Library by eye.

Demo dataset, built to test the framework. View the code on GitHub →
